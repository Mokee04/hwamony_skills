# Hwamony Service Naming Copy

Turn a service brief into market-aware names, shortlist candidates, and launch-ready copy.

This skill is for naming work that has to survive contact with the real market. It reads a brief, researches current competitor and category language, explores multiple naming lanes, shortlists stronger candidates, and writes launch-ready copy for the best options.

## 🚀 Start Here

- `Use $hwamony-service-naming-copy to name this AI workflow product, show me Korean and English lanes, and write launch copy for the top 2 options.`
- `Use $hwamony-service-naming-copy to critique these existing candidate names first, then propose stronger replacements grounded in the current market.`
- `Use $hwamony-service-naming-copy to turn this rough service memo into 8 serious name candidates, a recommended winner, and hero copy for the landing page.`

## ✨ Why It Is Different

- it does live category research before recommending finalists
- it compares naming patterns to find overused language and whitespace
- it treats naming and launch copy as one decision, not two disconnected tasks
- it supports Korean, English, and hybrid directions without mixing them carelessly

## 🧭 Use It When

- you have a service brief, PRD, memo, landing page draft, or rough notes
- you want Korean, English, or hybrid naming directions
- you need tagline, headline, subheadline, positioning line, or CTA copy
- you want a critique of existing candidate names before replacing them

## 📦 What You Get

- a compact brief summary
- a research snapshot with category naming patterns
- finalists grouped by lane or language
- strengths, risks, and rough uniqueness notes
- copy for the top candidates
- a recommended winner, second-best alternative, and next validation step

## 🔎 What To Validate Next

- whether the winner is too close to a strong incumbent in the same category
- whether the name stays pronounceable and memorable in the target market
- whether the copy still works once the landing page or deck gets more concrete
- whether legal or trademark screening is needed before committing

## 🧠 Best Fit

- teams naming a new service, app, SaaS, feature, or brand
- users who want names grounded in the current market instead of pure intuition
- cases where the final recommendation needs both names and copy that fit the same positioning

## ⚠️ Boundary

This skill is strongest when naming and positioning belong together. If the job is only headline tweaking on an already-final name, a lighter copy pass may be enough.
