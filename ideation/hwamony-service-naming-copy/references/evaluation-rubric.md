# Naming Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to score finalists before presenting them.

## Scorecard

Rate each candidate from 1 to 5 on:

- distinctiveness: Is it meaningfully different from competitors?
- clarity: Does it hint at the category, outcome, or feeling without needing a long explanation?
- pronounceability: Can the target audience say it correctly after seeing it once?
- memorability: Does it stick after a short delay?
- stretch: Can the brand grow beyond a single feature or narrow use case?
- searchability: Is it likely to be discoverable without extreme ambiguity?
- risk: Are there obvious collision, confusion, or negative-language issues?

Do not present raw scores alone. Add one short rationale and one red-flag note per candidate.

## Elimination Rules

Drop a candidate when any of the following is true:

- it is too similar to a strong incumbent in the same category
- it is difficult to spell, hear, or pronounce in the target market
- it depends on a trend word that already feels saturated
- it sounds clever but fails to communicate the promised outcome or emotional direction

## Copy Angles

Choose one primary angle per winning candidate:

- outcome-first: emphasize the end benefit
- problem-first: emphasize the pain being removed
- identity-first: emphasize who the user becomes
- speed-first: emphasize simplicity or momentum
- trust-first: emphasize reliability, expertise, or enterprise confidence

Keep the naming rationale and copy angle aligned. A playful metaphor name should not be paired with stiff, legalistic launch copy unless the user explicitly wants that contrast.
