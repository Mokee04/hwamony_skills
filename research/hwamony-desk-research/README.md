# Hwamony Desk Research

Turn one-shot searching into staged desk research with saved sources, checkpoints, and reusable artifacts.

This skill is for questions that need more than one search pass. It scopes the topic, sets a source plan, researches in batches with checkpoints, and saves the work as a reusable project folder with preserved source texts, findings, and citations.

## 🚀 Start Here

- `Use $hwamony-desk-research to turn this vague question about the AI coding assistant market in Korea into a scoped research folder with saved sources and a final memo.`
- `Use $hwamony-desk-research to build a source-backed memo on this policy question and preserve the raw source text so we can audit it later.`
- `Use $hwamony-desk-research to run a competitor scan on this category, checkpoint after the first batch, and keep the source files on disk.`

## ✨ Why It Is Different

- it scopes before searching instead of rewarding vague research requests
- it works in batches with checkpoints instead of trying to finish everything in one pass
- it preserves source text files so findings stay auditable later
- it produces a reusable research folder, not just a polished final answer

## 🧭 Use It When

- you need a market or category scan
- you want a source-backed memo or briefing
- you need competitor, policy, or landscape research
- you want research artifacts saved to disk instead of buried in chat

## 📦 What You Get

- a research brief
- batch-by-batch findings
- preserved source files
- final findings and bibliography

## 🧠 Best Fit

- market, policy, category, and competitor questions that are still too broad
- users who need citations, source preservation, and work they can revisit later
- topics where “just search and summarize” is too shallow or too fragile

## ⚠️ Boundary

This skill is for turning a fuzzy research ask into a scoped project with decisions, checkpoints, and preserved evidence. If the question is already narrow and only needs a quick factual answer, a lighter research pass may be enough.
