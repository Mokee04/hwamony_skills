# Research Skills

This folder contains skills for exploring a topic, gathering material, and producing structured outputs from that exploration.

Some skills in this category are evidence-oriented. Others are designed to test a topic by putting multiple viewpoints into a structured debate format.

## Included Skills

### `hwamony-desk-research`

Staged desk research with scoped questions, source strategy, batch-based progress, and saved research artifacts.

Use this when you want:

- a market or category scan
- a trend or policy overview
- a competitor or landscape review
- a source-backed briefing memo
- preserved source files and documented research steps

### `hwamony-debate-panel`

A multi-agent debate workflow for exploring a question through opposing perspectives.

Use this when you want:

- a topic narrowed into a cleaner debate resolution
- multiple personas arguing different sides
- a moderated structure instead of one assistant improvising both sides
- saved prompts and transcripts for later follow-up

This skill includes a `.env.example` file because live runs may require API keys.

## What This Category Is Good At

- clarifying a topic before going deep
- making research more inspectable and reproducible
- preserving outputs as reusable artifacts
- surfacing tensions, tradeoffs, and competing arguments

## Good Fit

- researchers and analysts
- founders exploring a market
- teams preparing a memo or brief
- users who want more than a one-shot answer

## Working Style

The skills here favor process over guesswork.

That means:

- narrowing scope before running
- documenting what was done
- saving outputs for later reuse
- making assumptions visible
