# Hwamony CBT Chats

Guide safe, structured CBT-style conversations without drifting into diagnosis or crisis care.

## What It Does

This skill helps users examine a recent situation in CBT terms: the situation, automatic thoughts, evidence for and against a thought, a more balanced thought, and a small next step. It stays practical, collaborative, and safety-aware.

## Why It Is Different

- it is structured enough to be useful without sounding like a worksheet dump
- it stays non-diagnostic and safety-aware in high-risk territory
- it focuses on one concrete example instead of vague emotional generalities
- it is better for careful reframing and next-step design than generic comfort talk

## Best For

- users who want to examine one recent situation carefully instead of talking in generalities
- moments when gentle structure is more useful than either pure reassurance or a long worksheet
- people who want a thought record, balanced reframe, and one small next step

## Not Therapy Roleplay

This skill is not trying to imitate licensed treatment or improvise a diagnosis.

It is strongest when someone wants a careful CBT-style conversation around one specific situation, one automatic thought, the evidence around it, and a small next step that feels believable.

## Use It When

- you want a CBT-style thought record
- you want to examine an automatic thought more carefully
- you want a gentle but structured check-in
- you want a small behavioral step instead of abstract advice

## Boundaries

- not a licensed-therapy replacement
- not for crisis support
- not for diagnosis
- not for overconfident interpretations of trauma or personality

## Example Prompts

- `Use $hwamony-cbt-chats to help me work through one stressful situation from this week without drifting into generic reassurance.`
- `Use $hwamony-cbt-chats to build a thought record for this automatic thought and check the evidence for and against it.`
- `Use $hwamony-cbt-chats to help me reframe this thought and choose one very small next step that I could actually do today.`
- `Use $hwamony-cbt-chats to help me unpack this moment from yesterday, find the automatic thought, and land on a more balanced version.`
