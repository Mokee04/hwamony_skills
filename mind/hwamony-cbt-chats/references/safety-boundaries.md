# Safety Boundaries

## Stop Normal CBT Flow When

- the user expresses intent or plan for self-harm or suicide
- the user expresses intent to harm someone else
- abuse, severe neglect, or immediate danger is present
- there may be an acute medical emergency
- the user appears highly disorganized, psychotic, or unable to follow a basic structured conversation safely

## Response Pattern

1. acknowledge the urgency briefly
2. stop the CBT worksheet flow
3. encourage immediate local crisis, emergency, or professional support
4. keep the response practical and direct

## Do Not

- continue cognitive restructuring as if it were a routine case
- debate whether the user is really in danger
- claim to provide emergency help

## Geographic Caution

Do not assume the user's country unless context makes it clear.

- If the user appears to be in the United States or explicitly wants US resources, mention `988` and `911` for immediate danger.
- Otherwise, encourage the user to contact local emergency services, a local crisis line, or a nearby trusted person who can stay with them right now.

## Example Korean Crisis Redirect

```text
지금은 일반적인 CBT 방식으로 계속 정리하는 것보다, 바로 안전을 우선하는 게 더 중요해 보여요.

혼자 버티지 말고 지금 바로 가까운 사람이나 지역 응급/위기 지원에 연락해줘요. 만약 지금 당장 위험이 있으면 지역 응급 서비스에 바로 연락해야 해요.
```

## Example English Crisis Redirect

```text
This looks like a situation where safety matters more than continuing a normal CBT-style conversation.

Please reach out to local emergency or crisis support right now, or contact a trusted person who can stay with you. If you are in the US or Canada, call or text 988. If there is immediate danger, call emergency services now.
```
