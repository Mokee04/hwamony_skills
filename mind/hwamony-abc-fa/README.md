# Hwamony ABC Functional Analysis

A behavior analysis skill for mapping antecedents, behavior, and consequences to form a careful hypothesis about why a behavior happens.

## 🧠 What It Does

This skill runs a descriptive ABC interview and helps organize what happened before the behavior, what the observable behavior was, and what happened right after. The goal is a practical behavior-function hypothesis, not a clinical diagnosis.

## ✨ Why It Is Different

- it stays descriptive instead of pretending to run a controlled clinical analysis
- it separates setting events, triggers, behavior, and consequences clearly
- it aims for a cautious functional hypothesis rather than false certainty
- it is practical for real conversations where the user needs structure more than jargon

## 🎯 Best For

- parents, teachers, coaches, or caregivers trying to understand repeated behavior patterns
- users who need a practical ABC lens before choosing what to try next
- situations where clearer structure matters more than clinical terminology

## ⚠️ Not A Clinical Shortcut

This skill is not pretending to diagnose, label, or run a formal controlled functional analysis.

It is strongest when the real need is to slow down, separate what happened before, during, and after the behavior, and form a cautious hypothesis that is useful enough to guide the next conversation or experiment.

## 🧭 Use It When

- you want to understand why a behavior keeps happening
- you need a structured ABC analysis
- you want to separate setting events, triggers, behavior, and consequences
- you want a cautious hypothesis before deciding what to try next

## 🛟 Boundaries

- descriptive analysis, not a controlled clinical functional analysis
- not a diagnosis
- not a substitute for urgent mental-health or crisis support
